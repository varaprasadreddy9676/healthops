# Quick Tour

A one-line description of every top-level page.

## Operate

- **Dashboard** — health-at-a-glance: status counts, open incidents, recent uptime and latency.
- **Servers** — inventory of hosts HealthOps knows about plus their live signals.
- **Checks** — every monitor you have configured, with its current state and recent history.
- **Incidents** — open and historical problems with evidence and timeline.
- **Notifications** — delivery history and channel configuration.

## Detect

- **Log Events** — application and server log messages grouped into recurring patterns.
- **MySQL** — database connection, slow-query, and lock signals.
- **Heartbeats** — external jobs ping HealthOps; if they go silent, an incident opens.
- **Alert Rules** — thresholds that decide when a failing check becomes an incident.
- **Maintenance Windows** — silence checks during planned work.

## Understand

- **Analytics** — longer-window uptime, latency percentiles, MTTR, noisy monitors.
- **Monitor Tuning** — recommendations for reducing alert noise and improving coverage.
- **Status Pages** — public-facing pages that communicate service status.
- **Custom Dashboards** — your own layouts of charts and lists.

## AI (only visible when a provider is configured)

- **Root Cause** — AI-assisted analysis of an incident's evidence.
- **Ask AI** — conversational assistant for HealthOps data.
- **AI Results** — saved AI outputs.
- **Remediation** — guided actions for known incident types.

## Admin

- **Users** — accounts, roles, password reset.
- **Settings** — global configuration: AI providers, security, retention.
- **API Quickstart** — call HealthOps from your own scripts and services.

If a page is not in your left navigation, your account role or the AI configuration probably hides it. Admins see everything.
